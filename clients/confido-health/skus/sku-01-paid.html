What we collect, with your data not our guesses.

Sample finding (anonymized from prior healthtech engagements): Of 5 calls, AEs hit criteria 01–04 well (avg 4/5), but 05–08 (committee, timeline, champion) avg 1.4/5. Translation: AEs are running discovery against the obvious buyer and missing the people who actually decide.

→ CRM scan

Read-only access to last 12 months of opportunities. Run the 30-question scorecard against actual CRM data, not founder perception. Output:

  • Stage-conversion benchmark table (% closing through each stage, by buyer subtype)
  • Avg deal size by segment, with median and P90 (variance matters at Series A)
  • Win/loss rate by buyer type and competitor (which deals you actually lose, to whom)
  • Time-in-stage outliers — deals stuck >45 days at any stage flagged for re-grade
  • Forecast accuracy — last 4 quarters' forecast vs. actual, by AE if multiple

→ Win/loss interviews

3–5 calls — 2 wins, 2 losses, 1 stalled. 30 min each. Standard question set:

  1. Walk me through the day you decided to start looking for a vendor like Confido.
  2. Who else made the shortlist? What knocked them off?
  3. What were the 2 things Confido said that earned the meeting? The 1 thing that almost killed the deal?
  4. If you could change one thing about how the deal got worked, what would it be?
  5. (Loss) What did the winner do that we didn't? Be brutal.
  6. (Stalled) What changed inside your org that paused this? When does it un-pause?
