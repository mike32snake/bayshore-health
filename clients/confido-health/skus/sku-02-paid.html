§ Sample · Section 07 · Discovery script

Six mandatory questions. One persona variant per.

Discovery rigor is what separates 30% conversion from 60%. Every enterprise discovery call must produce a quoted answer (not the AE's interpretation) to all six. Below: each question, what listening for, persona variant for VP Patient Access (the most common Confido sponsor).

— Q1 —
Volume — "What's your annual inbound call volume across all sites?"

VP Patient Access variant: "Walk me through a typical Monday morning at the front desk. How many calls hit before noon? What's your team's coverage look like at 11am?" → Listening: hard number, plus the qualitative "Mondays are brutal" admission.

— Q2 —
Abandon — "What's your current call abandon rate, and what's your target?"

VP Patient Access variant: "What does your dashboard show for abandon rate this quarter? When did you last move that number?" → Listening: a number with a target (functional), no answer (red flag — they don't measure), or the answer + "we've tried 3 things and nothing moved it" (perfect setup).

— Q3 —
Spend — "What are you paying today for IVR, answering service, or outbound dialer? Hard number."

VP Patient Access variant: "Walk me through every line item on your patient-access tech stack. Names, vendors, dollars." → Listening: complete or incomplete answer. Incomplete = the CFO doesn't know either, which is its own selling point ("we'd consolidate this").

— Q4 —
Decision authority — "Who signs the contract? Does this need a steering committee?"

VP Patient Access variant: "If we agreed today, what's the path to a signed contract? Does your CIO need to bless it, or can you sign?" → Listening: a process they've walked before (good — a real path) vs. a process they're inventing (warning — long cycle).

— Q5 —
Timeline — "If we agreed today, when would you want to be live? What forces that date?"

VP Patient Access variant: "Is there a moment in 2026 when you absolutely need this working? A budget cycle, a survey period, a leadership review?" → Listening: a real forcing function (good) or "soon" (no urgency = no deal yet).

— Q6 —
Champion test — "Who in your org will lose their job if this doesn't work? Whose name is on it?"

VP Patient Access variant: "If we deploy and it works as well as it did at York Hospital, whose performance review benefits? If it goes badly, whose career takes the hit?" → Listening: a name comes back (deal at 70%+ confidence) or no name (deal at 30% — the champion isn't real).

Bayshore would build the same 6-question script with persona variants for: CIO (technical depth + integration risk variant), CMIO (clinician-experience + change-management variant), Owner-Dentist (4-question SMB script — much shorter), FQHC COO (governance-heavy variant).
