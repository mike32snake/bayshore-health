§ Sample · Objection matrix · 6 of 15

The actual pushbacks Confido AEs will hear, answered.

The full matrix has 15 objections sourced from win/loss interviews. Below: 6 high-confidence objections you'll hear in the voice-AI-for-healthcare space, with the answer + the customer story behind each. Categorized by what's actually being protected (cost · risk · integration · change management · security · politics).

Risk · most common
We tried IVR and patients hated it.

Voice AI isn't IVR. IVR is keypad menus + recorded prompts. Voice AI is conversational — the patient doesn't realize they're not talking to a person. The metric that matters is "abandon-after-greeting" — what % of callers hang up after hearing the system. IVR averages 30%. Modern voice AI averages under 8%.

Story: York Hospital deployment data — abandon-after-greeting dropped from 22% (legacy IVR) to 4% (Confido) in 30 days. We can show you the call recordings.
Risk · second most common
What happens when the AI gets it wrong?

Configurable escalation matrix. Any unrecognized intent or low-confidence response routes to a human in <3 seconds. Worst case = same as a busy receptionist transferring a call, but logged + auditable. Walk me through one of your recent worst-case calls — we'll show you exactly how it gets handled.

Story: Dallas Renal had a hard requirement that any call mentioning "chest pain" route to a clinical line within 2 seconds. Confido was configured to that workflow on day 1; it has triggered 14 times in 6 months, all routed correctly.
Integration · CIO will ask
My CIO won't approve another vendor.

Pre-built integrations with 11 EHRs (Athena, eCW, Dentrix, ModMed, Practice Fusion, etc.) — no custom build. SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + HIPAA from day one with a BAA pre-lawyered. Most CIOs sign in 2 weeks once they see the integration architecture and the security pack. We'd be glad to give them direct access to those documents this week.

Story: The York Hospital CIO had blocked 3 prior voice-AI vendors. Approved Confido in 12 days because we showed him the BAA + the audit-log access + the data-residency statement before he asked.
Cost · CFO will ask
How does this pencil out vs. just hiring more MAs?

$45K fully-loaded MA cost. Average 14-month tenure before turnover. ~$15K replacement cost per hire. Voice AI: $30–60K/year, no turnover. Replaces 0.7–1.2 FTE depending on call volume. Payback inside 8–12 months for most clinics. We'll co-build the math live with your CFO using your numbers, not ours — that's section 8 of the proposal.

Story: Good Shepherd FQHC was hiring 2 MAs/quarter before deployment. Net-new hires after deployment: zero in 6 months. Their CHRO presented the labor savings to the board.
Change management · COO will ask
Our front-desk staff will think we're trying to replace them.

Reframe internally before deployment: "Sara handles the routine calls so you can focus on the patients in front of you." Most staff thank the system within 30 days because they're no longer drowning. We'll share the internal communications template that worked at York and Dallas Renal.

Story: Dallas Renal staff initially anxious. Survey at 60 days: 87% reported "more enjoyable workday" and zero job losses (the goal was always burnout reduction, not headcount cuts).
Politics · CMIO will ask
Our last AI vendor over-promised. Why are you different?

We close on FTE replacement and abandon rate metrics — both inside 30 days. If we don't hit them, you don't pay for renewal. That's in the contract. Most vendors close on "you'll see results over time." We close on "if results aren't there in 30 days, you walk." We can put you in touch with three CMIOs at our customers who'll tell you whether we delivered or not.

Story: York Hospital CMIO had been burned by an ambient scribe vendor 2 years prior. Confido's 30-day metric guarantee was the contract clause that earned the pilot.

§ Sample · Demo script branches

One demo. Four branches. The AE picks the lane in the first 90 seconds.

The same demo flow with different emphasis based on persona. The AE asks one qualifier question at minute 1 ("what brought you to this call today?") and routes accordingly.

Branch · CIO
Lead with integration architecture, security, vendor consolidation

Persona is protecting against integration risk and vendor sprawl.

Open: "Before we touch the product — let me walk you through how we sit on top of your EHR." 5 minutes on the integration architecture, the BAA, the SOC 2 + ISO + audit log structure. Then show the product. Close: "If we sign tomorrow, your team's involvement is ~6 hours total over the 30-day onboard. Your IT team's involvement is the BAA review."

Branch · CMIO
Lead with clinical workflow continuity + change management

Persona is protecting against clinician disruption.

Open: "Walk me through what an average clinical day looks like for your nurses on the call line." Then demo Lilly (care gaps + transitions), since CMIOs care most about closing the loop. Mid-demo: pivot to the staff-experience data — "here's what your front-desk staff said at York after 60 days." Close: "We'd structure the rollout around your 30/60/90 clinician feedback cadence."

Branch · VP Patient Access
Lead with abandon rate, call volume, FTE math

Persona is the actual sponsor — feels the pain daily.

Open: "Walk me through your dashboard. Abandon rate, hold time, FCR — what numbers are you presenting at your weekly with the CMO?" Then demo Sara (appointment + payment) live with sample call recordings. Mid-demo: ROI calculator using their real call volume. Close: "If we deploy 30 days from today, what does your dashboard look like at day 60? Let's mock it up."

Branch · Owner-Dentist or Practice Owner (SMB motion)
Lead with revenue recovery + owner time recovered

Persona is wearing 4 hats including reception.

Open: "How many hours a week are you personally on the phone? What's that doing to your evening?" Demo Sara live, very short. Pivot to the recovered-no-show math: "Your no-show rate is 15%, your appointment is worth $180. Let me show you what we'd recover for you." Close: "30-day pilot, $499 for the first month, you decide at day 30 whether it's worth keeping."
