§ The honest framing

Why payor GTM isn't your next move.

Bayshore's Payor GTM Bundle is built for one specific motion: a digital-health vendor (typically clinical AI, RPM, behavioral health) trying to land a contract directly with a health plan. The buyer is a Chief Medical Officer or Chief Population Health Officer at a Blues plan or national MA carrier. The payment model is PMPM or shared-savings. The cycle is 9–18 months and committee-heavy.

That's not Confido. Confido's product enables provider organizations to do admin work better. Providers pay you; payors are downstream of your customer's revenue cycle, not your buyer. There's a path where Confido eventually sells to payors (the CMS Medicare Advantage AI rule we flagged in the competitive teardown opens a "verification & explanation" use case for plans), but that's 18–24 months out, not now.

What is your move: the same engagement engine — deep buyer playbook + named warm intros + reference architecture + security/legal pre-empt — but pointed at health system enterprise pursuits. The CIO of a top-25 IDN is exactly the kind of buyer the Payor Bundle was designed to navigate. We just changed which committee, which regulatory frame, which fee structure. Same engine. Different track.

§ How a Top-25 IDN actually buys voice AI

Five stages. Most vendors die at stage 3.

The motion the Health System Enterprise Pursuit Bundle is built to navigate. Stage 3 (security & contracting) is where 60% of healthtech-to-IDN deals die — same pattern as healthtech-to-payor.

— Stage 0 —
Pattern matching

System isn't shopping. CMIO or VP PA is watching the category. Confido enters consideration via peer adoption, regulator nudge (CPT-2026), or competitor failure. Months -6 to 0.

— Stage 1 —
Sponsor identified

One exec champions Confido internally. Almost never the contract signer — usually CMIO, VP Innovation, VP Patient Access. They need: 1-pager, ROI math, peer-IDN proof. Months 0 to 2.

— Stage 2 —
Committee forms

Around the sponsor: actuary, contracting, IT security, network ops, finance, legal. Treating sponsor as the buyer = lose. Each committee member has a kill switch. Months 2 to 5.

— Stage 3 —
Security & contracting

60% die here. 300-question security questionnaire. 12-week legal redline. BAA negotiation across 6 PHI layers. Most vendors aren't prepared. Months 5 to 8.

— Stages 4–5 —
Pilot → enterprise

Single market, single product line, single risk segment. Quantitative success criteria pre-negotiated. Pilot success doesn't auto-trigger enterprise — that's its own pitch. Months 8 to 24.

→ The 3 things that make this Confido's move, not someday-Confido's move

  • You already have York Hospital. One enterprise reference is the unlock. Without it, you can't get into Stage 1. With it, every stage is faster.
  • Your security posture is past the bar. SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + HIPAA from day one means Stage 3 is shorter for Confido than for ~80% of competitors. The "12-week redline" can be 4 weeks.
  • Adam's network is dense in IDN buyers. 220+ executive ecosystem includes 20+ named contacts at Top-25 IDNs — exactly the people who become Stage 1 sponsors.
