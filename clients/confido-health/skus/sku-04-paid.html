§ Sample · Buying committee map · Top-25 IDN archetype

Seven roles. Each with a way to kill the deal.

This is the "committee dynamics" map most playbooks skip. Confido needs to know not just who buys, but who blocks — and what specifically each one is protecting against.

— Signs —
CIO

Protects against integration risk + vendor sprawl + security exposure. Won't sign without IT Sec sign-off + an integration architecture review. Stage 4 gate.

— Champions —
CMIO

Cares about clinician experience + EHR workflow continuity + change management. Most likely Stage 1 sponsor. Without a CMIO champion, deal doesn't enter committee.

— Sponsors (feels pain) —
VP Patient Access

Measured on abandon rate, FCR, hold-time SLA, patient satisfaction. The person who actually wants Confido. Brings it to the committee. Treat as primary buyer in discovery.

— Can kill deal —
IT Security

Cares about SOC 2 Type 2 evidence, BAA terms, data residency, audit-log access. Will demand 60–300 question security questionnaire. Pre-empt with security pack in week 1.

— ROI gate —
CFO

Won't approve without 12-month payback math. Wants OpEx vs. CapEx breakdown + comparison to in-house IVR upgrade. Often the one who quietly says no after CMIO already said yes.

— Executes —
Practice Operations

The user. Cares about handoff to EHR, escalation paths, agent failure modes. Critical for change management. Their feedback at week 2 of pilot determines enterprise upgrade.

— Procurement —
VP / Director of Procurement

Cares about contract terms, vendor classification, pricing benchmarks, MSA boilerplate. Often inserts itself only at Stage 4. Have your MSA lawyer-ready.

— Clinical risk —
Chief Nursing Officer

Quiet veto. If voice AI screws up a patient interaction with clinical implications, they're the executive accountable. Brief them early or they'll kill it late.

— Strategic —
Innovation / Venture arm

Some IDNs route AI vendors through an innovation lab first. Useful as accelerator; dangerous if it becomes the only path (long cycle, no commitment to deploy).

§ Sample · Security questionnaire pre-fill index

The 60 questions Confido will get asked. Answers ready before they ask.

Most healthtech vendors take 4–8 weeks to respond to an IDN security questionnaire. Bayshore pre-fills 60 of the most common questions in advance, organized by category, so the AE has answers ready in 24 hours of the request. Below: the index of categories + sample question type per category.

→ The 60-question index, by category
  1. Identity & access management (8 questions) — sample: "Describe your MFA enforcement policy for production system access."
  2. Data residency & encryption (7 questions) — sample: "Where is patient PHI stored geographically? Provide a data flow diagram."
  3. SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / HIPAA evidence (6 questions) — sample: "Provide most recent SOC 2 Type 2 attestation letter and HITRUST certification if applicable."
  4. BAA & legal terms (5 questions) — sample: "Provide your standard BAA template. Identify any clauses that are non-negotiable."
  5. Incident response (6 questions) — sample: "Describe your incident response process. Provide notification SLAs for breach scenarios."
  6. Vendor risk management (4 questions) — sample: "List all sub-processors handling PHI, their roles, and your due diligence cadence."
  7. AI / model governance (8 questions — newer category) — sample: "Describe how you handle model bias monitoring and false-positive escalation in voice AI."
  8. Audit & logging (5 questions) — sample: "Describe customer access to audit logs. Retention period? Export formats?"
  9. Business continuity / disaster recovery (5 questions) — sample: "Provide RTO and RPO for production systems. Describe BC/DR testing cadence."
  10. EHR integration security (6 questions) — sample: "Describe authentication method for each EHR integration. Token rotation policy?"
Why this matters: 60% of healthtech-to-IDN deals die at Stage 3 (security & contracting). Vendors that pre-fill arrive at Stage 3 with 4–6 weeks of friction already removed. The pre-fill index alone often shaves 30 days off the cycle.
