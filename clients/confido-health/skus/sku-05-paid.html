§ Sample · Comp set · public pricing data points

Six vendors. What's actually published. What's actually paid.

Bayshore-anonymized in the final deliverable; named here for the free read. Each vendor's pricing model + price points (where public) + what we infer about real ACV from press, valuation rounds, and category-norm benchmarks.

Hyro AI

Enterprise quote-based · ~$95M raised · 45+ health systems
→ Pricing model
Custom enterprise quote per health system. Per-call or per-patient implied; not publicly disclosed.
→ Price points
None public. Inferred ACV based on 45+ health system deployments + $95M raised: $150K–$1M+ per system, with multi-hospital systems at upper end.
→ Implication for Confido
Hyro defines the enterprise ceiling for the category. Confido's Premium tier should anchor close to Hyro's lower-band, with differentiation on speed-to-deploy.

Suki AI

Per-user/month · clinical scribing
→ Pricing model
Suki Compose: $299/user/month. Suki Assistant: $399/user/month. Enterprise: custom.
→ Example ACV
5-physician clinic on Suki Assistant = $1,995/month = $23,940/year ACV.
→ Implication for Confido
Per-seat works for clinician-facing tools (Suki). Wrong unit for admin voice AI. Don't copy this model.

Talkdesk Healthcare

Per-user/month · contact center
→ Pricing model
$85–$225/user/month across plans. Healthcare Experience Cloud starts at $225/user/month. Telecom usage fees separate.
→ Typical annual
$30K–$250K+ depending on scale. 3-year contract assumed.
→ Implication for Confido
Cautionary tale on per-seat for contact-center automation. Buyer hates "we bought 5 seats but only 3 are active." Differentiate with per-call or PMPM.

Assort Health

Subscription · tiered by org size · ~$50M Series B at $750M
→ Pricing model
$1,500–$10,000+ per month, specialty-driven.
→ ARR signal
~$3M ARR at $750M valuation (Series B Aug 2025). Average ACV ~$30–60K.
→ Implication for Confido
Closest peer in pricing structure. Assort's $1.5–10K/month range is what your Tier 1 + Tier 2 should look like — but with a clearer scaling step-function (Assort's gap between bottom and top is too wide).

Hello Patient

Subscription · enterprise implied · $22.5M Series A
→ Pricing model
Not public. Enterprise subscription implied based on customer profile and Series A scale.
→ Inferred ACV
$60K–$200K typical based on Series A burn-rate math + customer count signal.
→ Implication for Confido
Direct competitor in patient-front-door space. Confido needs published pricing to differentiate from Hello Patient's "contact us" opacity — transparency is a wedge.

SuperDial

Per-completed-call · $15M Series A
→ Pricing model
Per-completed-call usage-based. Competitive vs. $2–$10 per call traditional call centers. Per-call price not publicly disclosed.
→ Differentiation
RCM focus (payor calls, insurance verification, denials) — adjacent, not direct competitor.
→ Implication for Confido
Validates per-completed-call model for healthcare voice AI. Worth offering as overage pricing model on top of base subscription.

→ Three packaging archetypes that emerged

  • Per-seat / per-user subscription (Suki, Talkdesk, Klara) — works for clinician-facing tools, fails for admin automation. Buyer rejects "seats" framing for non-seat value.
  • Enterprise subscription with custom quotes (Hyro, Notable, Memora, Infinitus, Hello Patient) — most common in voice AI. Higher ACV but opaque, slow sales cycle, hides true cost.
  • Usage-based / per-call (SuperDial, Intercom Fin) — emerging, rare in healthcare. Aligns vendor incentives but CFOs prefer fixed budgets.

Three packages. Real anchors.

Standard — Growth (recommended core)

$2,999–$15K/mo · $60–180K ACV
→ Multi-site specialty (10–50 providers) · FQHC 50K–200K patient panel · regional urgent care chain

The core tier. Where 70% of your ACV will live.

Pricing model
Option A: $2,999/mo for 10K calls/month + $0.15/call overage. Or Option B: $4,999/mo for 100K active patients + $0.35 PMPM overage. Buyer picks the unit they think in.
In tier
All 3 agents (Sara · Ryan · Lilly) · payment collection · pre/post-op + care gaps · multi-location hub · 2+ EHR integrations · 4–6 wk implementation · biweekly check-ins
Not in tier
Revenue-share model · custom workflows · dedicated CSM · white-label · 24/7 SLA

Premium — Enterprise

$25K–$100K+/mo · $300K–$1M+ ACV
→ Top-25 IDN · large dental DSO · academic medical center · multi-hospital network

Custom. Outcome-based component. Earned through Health System Enterprise Pursuit (SKU 04) motion.

Pricing model
Custom annual commitment based on patient population + call volume forecast · $0.08–$0.12/call overage (volume discount) · 10–15% of contract value tied to KPIs (no-show rate, automation rate, recovered revenue)

Full agent suite · unlimited integrations · white-label option · dedicated CSM + Engineering · 8–12 wk implementation · live training for 100+ staff

Outcome-based component is the moat. Most competitors won't price this way at Series A. Bayshore POV: launch this in Q2 2026 to be first-mover on value-based pricing.

§ Sample · Board-ready deck outline · 16 slides

What we hand the founder for the next board meeting.

The "we should change pricing" conversation is hard without slide-level scaffolding. Bayshore delivers a 14–18 slide deck the founder presents directly — board-tested format. Below: the 16-slide structure we'd build for Confido.

— 01 —
Where we are today: pricing snapshot

Single model, applied across 4 buyer subtypes. Median deal size, median discount, win rate by tier.

— 02 —
The pricing leak: 3 numbers

(1) % of deals with >15% discount · (2) ACV variance across buyer subtypes · (3) deals declined for being "too cheap to bother with"

— 03 —
Discount distribution analysis

Histogram: every closed deal in last 12 months by discount %. Where the median sits, where the long tail is. Most companies are surprised.

— 04 —
Win rate by attempted tier

If we tried to sell "enterprise" at small specialty: what was the win rate? If "small" at hospital: what was the win rate? Tells us where pricing-model mismatch is killing deals.

— 05 —
Top 3 antipatterns we found

From the pricing forensics work — usually #1 (one price for every buyer) and #2 (per-seat or wrong unit) plus one company-specific.

— 06 —
Comp set: 6 vendors, anonymized

2D map: ACV vs. proof level (logos, RWE, security posture). Where Confido sits today; where we could sit with new pricing.

— 07 —
Comp set: implications

One slide: Hyro defines enterprise ceiling. Talkdesk shows per-seat failure mode. Assort defines mid-market band. SuperDial validates per-call model.

— 08 —
Recommended structure: Entry

$499/mo base + $0.25/patient overage. Target: small specialty/dental/rural FQHC. Expected ACV $6–8K. Expected gross margin 75–80%.

— 09 —
Recommended structure: Standard

$2,999–$15K/mo. Target: multi-site specialty/FQHC. Expected ACV $60–180K. Expected gross margin 70–75%. Where 70% of ACV will live.

— 10 —
Recommended structure: Premium

$25–100K+/mo with outcome-based component. Target: enterprise IDN. Expected ACV $300K–$1M+. Expected gross margin 60–65%.

— 11 —
Buyer ROI calculator: hospital

FTE replacement math + recovered abandon revenue. Live model the AE leaves with the buyer.

— 12 —
Buyer ROI calculator: specialty/dental

Recovered no-show revenue + owner-time recovered.

— 13 —
Pilot-to-enterprise step function

Pilot pricing + the 3 stated milestones that unlock enterprise tier. The clause that protects against pilot-priced-forever lock-in.

— 14 —
Rollout plan

What changes when (week 1: communicate to existing customers · week 2: AE training · week 4: new website pricing · week 6: first new-tier deal closed).

— 15 —
What we considered + rejected

Pure usage-based · revenue-share at Tier 1 · annual minimums on Entry · etc. The slide that prevents the board re-litigating choices we already analyzed.
